TIDELESS

Narrative life-sim set in a declining British seaside town. You have 14 days to earn enough money to pay off a debt. Work odd jobs, get to know the locals, decide who you spend your limited time with.

Genre: Narrative life-sim / survival-lite  |  Playtime: 3-5 hours  |  Engine: Unreal Engine 5

The Concept

"Tideless" refers to both the town and its people. The tide barely comes in anymore. The people don't leave. Everyone is stuck here for their own reasons: debt, family obligations, or just inertia.

You're here because of your brother's debt. He left, you're holding the bag. 14 days to pay it off or you're not leaving either.

You have to find work to earn enough to eat and chip away at the debt. The people you meet have their own stories, but you won't have time to know all of them properly. You pick who to invest in.

Tone

The town looks tired. Grey weather, closed shops, paint peeling off Victorian facades. The visual direction leans into this.

The contrast comes from the people. When you spend time with someone, the scenes shift warmer. Pub interiors, kitchen conversations, amber light through foggy windows. The game's colour palette reflects whether you're alone or with someone.

Characters

Five NPCs. Three work NPCs, one neighbour, one hidden character found through exploration. Relationship progression means you can realistically get close to one per playthrough.

Visual Direction

Primary reference is The Long Dark. Painterly textures with visible brushwork applied to 3D environments. Heavy atmospheric fog. Simplified shapes that read well as silhouettes. Colour grading shifts with context.

We're applying that approach to British coastal architecture and weather. The palette is different: desaturated teals and greys for exteriors, amber and warm yellow for interiors and social scenes.
